Week 1: Reverse Engineering Hidden Systems

Learning to think about the systems, decisions, and histories embedded in designed objects and digital artifacts.

Project preview

A playful overview of this week's make, showing the ideas, systems, and questions that drove the work. Keep an eye out for how the project surfaces hidden choices and shapes what the final result feels like.

The Artifact

This week, I selected an artifact to reverse engineer—examining the choices, systems, and assumptions embedded within it. By studying how it was made, what decisions shaped it, and what it reveals about the world it was created in, I learned to see technology not as natural or neutral, but as a product of specific human choices and constraints.

Process Notes

How did I make this? I examined the artifact carefully, documenting:

  • Its physical or digital structure
  • The tools and methods used to create it
  • The intended purpose and use cases
  • Hidden or implied assumptions
  • Who benefits from its design

What tools did I use? Close observation, research, and critical analysis.

What decisions did I make? I chose to focus on the choices that might be invisible at first glance— the small decisions that shape how people interact with the artifact and what it teaches them about the world.

Reflection

This assignment taught me that nothing is designed neutrally. Every object, system, and interface embodies choices about who it's for, what it's for, and what it assumes about the world. By learning to reverse engineer, I'm learning to read the world more critically—to see the human choices behind things that feel inevitable or natural.
