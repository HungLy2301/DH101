About

Who I Am

I'm Hung Ly, a student exploring the intersections of digital humanities, creative technology, and critical thinking. My work examines how systems—whether algorithmic, social, or infrastructural—shape meaning, identity, and experience.

Through courses like DH101, I've learned to think about technology not just as tools, but as sites of power, creativity, and cultural meaning. I believe that understanding how systems work is the first step toward building better ones.

My Approach

I work across multiple media and methods: from interactive narrative design to critical mapping, from text analysis to creative coding. What ties these practices together is a commitment to making visible what systems—especially those involving technology and power—tend to hide.

I use AI thoughtfully. I see it as a collaborator for ideation and structural thinking, but not a replacement for judgment, voice, or editorial decision-making. Every piece of work you see here reflects my own curation and perspective.

What I'm Interested In

  • AI labor, ethics, and environmental impact
  • Algorithmic systems and bias
  • Digital identity and self-representation
  • Narrative structures in interactive media
  • Data visualization and counter-mapping
  • The politics of infrastructure
  • Creative coding and generative systems
  • Digital literacy and critical thinking
